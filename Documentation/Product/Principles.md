# Product Principles

These principles guide trade-offs. A proposal that conflicts with them needs an
explicit rationale and review.

## 1. Data belongs to the user

Users must be able to access, export, and remove their financial data. Product
convenience does not override ownership.

## 2. Privacy is the default

Core functionality must not require external analytics, advertising SDKs, or
transmission of financial data. Optional network features require clear consent.

## 3. Offline is a complete mode

Creating, editing, searching, and analyzing local data must work without an
internet connection. Synchronization augments local storage; it does not replace
it.

## 4. Correctness before novelty

Totals, conversions, migrations, and destructive operations receive stronger
verification than decorative features.

## 5. Stability before feature count

A smaller trustworthy release is preferable to broad, partially reliable scope.

## 6. Daily actions stay fast

The common transaction flow should require minimal navigation and avoid fields
that are not needed for the current action.

## 7. Important information is immediately understandable

The primary view should answer the user's most likely questions without forcing
them to interpret dense tables or unexplained scores.

## 8. Guidance is calm and actionable

Avoid shame-based warnings. Explain what changed, why it matters, and what the
user can do next.

## 9. Intelligence is explainable

Forecasts and insights must reveal their inputs and assumptions. Rules and local
analysis come before opaque external AI.

## 10. Simplicity wins over spectacle

Animation, color, and custom components must improve comprehension or feedback.
They are not goals by themselves.

## 11. Accessibility is part of quality

Keyboard navigation, VoiceOver, contrast, Dynamic Type where applicable, Reduce
Motion, and clear localization are considered during design—not after release.

## 12. Accepted decisions change deliberately

New information can justify a change, but the reason, trade-offs, and migration
impact must be documented before an accepted foundation is replaced.
